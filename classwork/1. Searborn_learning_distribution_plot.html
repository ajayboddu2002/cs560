Distribution plot¶

Ref: https://seaborn.pydata.org/tutorial/distributions.html
Color palette: https://seaborn.pydata.org/tutorial/color_palettes.html

It enables us to visualize the distribution of the data set. The functions are:

  • displot() or histplot()
  • jointplot()
  • pairplot()
  • rugplot()
  • kdeplot()
In [12]:
#import seaborn
import seaborn as sns 
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
%matplotlib inline

displot() or histplot()¶

The most common approach to visualizing a distribution is the histogram. Figure-level interface for drawing distribution plots onto a FacetGrid.

In [13]:
sns.displot(tips['total_bill'])
plt.show()
In [16]:
sns.histplot(tips['total_bill'],kde=True) # kde: kernel density estimate
Out[16]:
<AxesSubplot:xlabel='total_bill', ylabel='Count'>

joint plot¶

Draw a plot of two variables with bivariate and univariate graphs with a variation of kind paramter. kind parameter can be set to:

  • scatter
  • kde
  • hex
  • reg etc.
In [20]:
#shows the relationship betten tip and total_bill
#kind='scatter'
sns.jointplot(x='total_bill',y='tip',data=tips,kind='scatter')
Out[20]:
<seaborn.axisgrid.JointGrid at 0x7fc01c1309d0>

kdeplot and rugpplot¶

-rugplot draws a dash mark for every point on a univariate distribution.

-KDE plot replaces every single observation with a Gaussian (Normal) distribution centered around that value

In [47]:
#sns.rugplot(tips['tip'])
sns.rugplot(tips['total_bill'])
Out[47]:
<AxesSubplot:xlabel='total_bill'>
In [51]:
sns.kdeplot(tips['total_bill'])
sns.rugplot(tips['total_bill'])
Out[51]:
<AxesSubplot:xlabel='total_bill', ylabel='Density'>
